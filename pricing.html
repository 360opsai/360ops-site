Atlas vs. Spartan — what's the same, what's different

Both run the same engine and scrub sensitive data before any cloud AI call — raw PII / PHI / CUI / financials never reach Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google on either. The difference is residency: Atlas stores data in cloud infrastructure (US); Spartan stores data on your own hardware. The same optional modules (Guard, Security, Pulse, Tech, Operation) can stack on top of either base. Choose Spartan when your compliance requirements mandate on-premises data residency, when your contracts prohibit cloud data storage, or when air-gap mode is required (DoD, classified work).

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before you choose a plan.

What's the difference between Personal, Atlas, and Spartan?
Personal (360ops Desktop) is the native AI app for individuals — runs on Windows / macOS / Linux. Solo $29.99 / Pro $49.99 / Power $99.99. Business · Atlas is the cloud-hosted multi-tenant Business OS at $99/user/mo (5-user minimum) with cloud AI included. Business · Spartan is the on-prem version of Atlas — same intelligence, your data never leaves your building. Optional modules (Guard, Security, Pulse, Tech, Operation) stack on top of any base subscription as add-ons. All three scrub sensitive data before any AI model sees it.
How does Atlas pricing work, and how do modules fit in?
Atlas is the cloud Business base subscription at $99/user/mo with a 5-user minimum. It includes cloud AI usage with all major providers (Claude, GPT, Gemini), Power-tier features, team-level RBAC, shared memory, and full integrations. Modules are optional add-ons that stack on top of your Atlas subscription: Guard (+$2.99/user), Security (+$9.99/user), Pulse (+$19.99/user), Tech (+$49.99/user), and Operation (+$299.99/mo flat per organization, Business only). You must have an Atlas (or Spartan) base to add any module.
Is cloud AI usage included or billed separately?
Cloud AI usage is included with every Atlas seat — Claude, GPT, and Gemini are all available through our managed routing, and the cost is bundled into the $99/user/mo. For teams with heavy usage that exceeds the included quota, we offer overage and refill options so your work never stops. No surprise bills — every overage prompts confirmation first.
Why is there no free tier?
Free tiers cost real money to support — API calls, infrastructure, and human time. Our entry prices ($29.99 Personal Solo, $99/user Atlas) are designed to be fair and to include real margin so we can support you properly. We'd rather charge you fairly and give you a great product than give you something free and unsupported.
What's the AI credits wallet?
The credits wallet is separate from your subscription. You add credits upfront — $50, $100, $250, or a custom amount — and they're consumed as your agents run AI queries. You get alerts at 50%, 20%, and 0% balance. When your wallet hits $0, AI pauses automatically. No surprise charges, ever.
Does Spartan require specific hardware?
Yes — Spartan requires compatible on-premises hardware that meets minimum compute specifications. We don't lock you to a specific manufacturer or model. Contact sales and we'll provide the minimum hardware specs based on your expected usage volume and which sovereignty mode you need. White-glove setup means we handle configuration end-to-end on your chosen hardware.
What are the 3 sovereignty modes on Spartan?
Connected (default) — scrubbed queries may reach cloud AI providers; raw data stays on the box. Restricted — PHI and CUI are forced to local-only models; other data may reach cloud after scrubbing. Air-gap — local models only; no outbound cloud AI calls. Used for DoD work and the highest-sensitivity contracts. You pick the mode at deployment; it's enforced at the network layer.
Do both Atlas and Spartan scrub data?
Yes — data scrubbing applies to both. On Atlas, scrubbing happens in our cloud infrastructure before queries reach AI providers. On Spartan, scrubbing happens on your hardware before any query leaves your premises. The key difference is residency: Atlas stores data in cloud (US), Spartan stores data on your hardware. Neither sends raw PII, PHI, CUI, or financial data to Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google.
Can I upgrade from Personal to Atlas?
Yes — and it's designed to be seamless. Your memory and personal settings carry over when you upgrade. When you're ready to bring your team onto a multi-user platform, one button in your dashboard initiates the upgrade to Atlas ($99/user/mo, 5-user minimum). You keep everything you've built.
Can I cancel anytime?
Yes, Personal and Atlas plans can be cancelled at any time. You keep access through the end of your billing period. Spartan requires a minimum 12-month commitment given the white-glove deployment investment. Any unused prepaid credits are refundable on cancellation.
